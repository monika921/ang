INVESTIGATED FACTORS THAT INFLUENCE THE HUMORAL AND CELLULAR VACCINE RESPONSE IN HUMANS INCLUDE:

  1. intrinsic host factors(such as age, sex, genetics, and comorbidities)
  2. prenatal factors(such as gestational age, birth weight, feeding method, and maternal factors)
  3. extrinsic factors(such as preexisting immunity, microbiota, infections, and antibiotics)
  4. environmental factors(such as geographic location, season, family size, and toxins)
  5. behavioral factors(such as smoking, alcohol consumption, exercise, and sleep)
  6. nutritional factors(such as body mass index, micronutrients, and enteropathy)
  7. vaccine factors(such vaccine type, product, adjuvant and dose)
  8. administration factors(schedule, site, route, time of vaccination, and coadministered vaccines and other drugs
